About the Center

A premier academic think-tank for Hong Kong’s digital asset economy

Established under the PolyU Faculty of Business, the Center for Digital Assets and Innovation (CDAI) is a dedicated research hub examining the technological, economic, and regulatory forces reshaping global finance. As Hong Kong consolidates its position as Asia’s leading virtual asset hub in 2026, CDAI provides the rigorous, independent scholarship needed to inform sound policy and responsible market development.

Our work sits at the convergence of three defining movements of the decade: the tokenization of real-world assets (RWA), the maturation of Web3 infrastructure, and the emergence of regulated stablecoins and the e-HKD. Bridging academia, industry, and regulators, the Center translates frontier research into actionable insight for the Hong Kong Monetary Authority, the SFC, financial institutions, and the broader FinTech ecosystem.

Grounded in PolyU’s tradition of applied, impact-driven scholarship, CDAI convenes economists, computer scientists, legal scholars, and practitioners to address the questions that will define the next generation of capital markets.

Research Pillars

Three domains shaping the future of finance

Our research agenda is organized around three interconnected pillars — each addressing a critical frontier in the transition toward a tokenized, decentralized, and digitally regulated financial system.

01

Digital Assets & Tokenization

Real-World Assets (RWA)

Investigating the on-chain tokenization of bonds, funds, real estate, and private credit — from valuation frameworks and market microstructure to settlement, custody, and the institutional infrastructure underpinning a multi-trillion-dollar asset class.

  • Tokenized fixed income & fund structures
  • On-chain settlement & custody models
  • Liquidity & secondary-market design
02

Web3 & Decentralized Finance

DeFi & On-Chain Markets

Examining the economics and governance of decentralized protocols — automated market makers, lending markets, and DAOs — alongside the systemic risks, smart-contract security, and incentive design that determine whether open finance can scale responsibly.

  • Protocol economics & token incentives
  • Smart-contract risk & security
  • Decentralized governance (DAOs)
03

RegTech & Digital Currencies

e-HKD & Stablecoins

Studying the design and policy implications of central bank digital currencies and regulated stablecoins — including the e-HKD pilot, Hong Kong’s stablecoin licensing regime, cross-border payments, and the RegTech tools enabling compliant, programmable money.

  • CBDC architecture & e-HKD pilots
  • Stablecoin regulation & reserves
  • AML/CFT & automated compliance
People Spotlight

Meet our core leadership

A distinguished team uniting academic rigour and industrial pragmatism to steer the Centre’s research, partnerships, and policy engagement.

Prof. Yue Feng

Director, Center for Digital Assets and Innovation
Assistant Dean, Faculty of Business, PolyU
Associate Professor, Department of Management and Marketing

Serving as the Chief Academic Leader of CDAI, Prof. Feng’s primary research focuses on the deployment and implications of Generative AI (GenAI) across consumer behavior patterns, digital marketing ecosystems, and emerging FinTech applications. Under her rigorous leadership, the Centre aggressively advances blockchain infrastructure, regulatory compliance frameworks, and real-world asset (RWA) tokenization. She spearheaded CDAI’s foundational multi-party industry MOUs and consistently advises on digital asset policy initiatives in Hong Kong.

Prof. Dan Tang

Advisor, Center for Digital Assets and Innovation
Professor of Practice, Faculty of Business, PolyU

Prof. Tang is a seasoned enterprise architect and pioneer in industrial blockchain scaling and tokenized assets digitization, widely recognized as the founder of YatChain and Octopus Bot systems. Bringing immense industrial pragmatism to the Centre, he steers applied research in tokenomics optimization, decentralized scaling frameworks, and robust stablecoin liquidity infrastructure. Prof. Tang aggressively champions academic knowledge transfer, frequently representing the Centre across flagship global investment summits and regulatory sandbox initiatives.
